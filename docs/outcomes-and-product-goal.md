# Outcomes and product goal

## Outcome statement

Enable conservation practitioners to make better, faster and more consistent decisions about survey targeting, site context and conservation prioritisation using spatial habitat suitability evidence.

## Product goal

Create a practical decision-support application that helps users explore habitat suitability outputs, understand the ecological drivers behind them, and apply those insights in real conservation and planning workflows.

## What success should look like

The product should help users:

- identify areas worth surveying or investigating further
- interpret a site in its wider landscape context
- explain why an area appears more or less suitable
- compare locations more consistently
- use spatial evidence more confidently and more transparently

## User impact we want

Users should be able to move from:

- static maps or fragmented data
- uncertain interpretation
- inconsistent spatial reasoning

to:

- interactive exploration
- clearer ecological interpretation
- better-supported planning and prioritisation decisions

## Product impact we want

The product should create value by making habitat suitability evidence:

- more accessible
- more interpretable
- more usable in practice
- easier to communicate
- easier to apply responsibly

## Non-goals

The product goal is not to:

- build a complex modelling platform for end users
- expose every technical detail of the underlying models
- automate planning or conservation decisions
- replace field expertise with algorithmic outputs

## Working product thesis

If we provide a clear, interpretable and spatially useful application for habitat suitability evidence, then practitioners will be better able to target effort, interpret place and prioritise action with more consistency and transparency.
