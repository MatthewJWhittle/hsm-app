# Problem statement

## Summary

Ecologists, bat groups and conservation practitioners need robust, spatially explicit tools to help them understand habitat suitability across the landscape, target survey effort more effectively, and prioritise conservation action. Existing evidence is often incomplete, unevenly distributed and difficult to interpret consistently at regional and local scales, making it hard to move from raw ecological data to practical, explainable decisions.

## Why this matters

Conservation and ecological decisions are often made with:

- limited survey time
- limited funding and capacity
- uneven ecological evidence
- patchy regional context
- reliance on static maps, raw records or individual expertise

This makes it difficult to answer practical questions such as:

- Where should future survey effort be focused?
- Which areas may be most important for conservation attention?
- How should a site be interpreted in its wider landscape context?
- Where do raw records suggest ecological value, and where do they simply reflect survey effort?

A stronger decision-support tool is needed to help practitioners work more consistently and make better use of available evidence.

## The core problem

The problem is not simply a lack of ecological data. It is that users need better ways to turn fragmented and uneven evidence into practical action.

Practitioners need tools that can help them:

- see beyond raw record density
- understand habitat suitability across a region
- investigate suitability at local scales
- compare areas more consistently
- support transparent decision-making with defensible spatial evidence

## Why existing evidence is not enough

Existing ecological evidence is valuable, but often difficult to use directly for landscape-scale decisions because it is:

- incomplete
- unevenly distributed
- influenced by where surveys have taken place
- difficult to compare consistently across large areas
- often disconnected from the day-to-day workflows of conservation practitioners

In practice, this means that potentially useful evidence does not always translate into usable insight.

## What kind of support is needed

The need is for robust tools that can support three broad types of work:

1. **Survey planning**
   - identify areas where future effort may add most value
   - provide regional context for field planning
   - highlight promising areas for further investigation

2. **Site and landscape interpretation**
   - understand how a site sits within the surrounding landscape
   - assess whether a location appears to be within a broader suitable context
   - compare places more transparently

3. **Conservation prioritisation**
   - identify areas that may warrant more attention
   - support strategic thinking across larger geographies
   - help move from record patterns to broader ecological interpretation

## What this project is trying to enable

This project exists to support more informed and more explainable ecological decision-making by providing stronger spatial tools for conservation and survey workflows.

The product should help users move from:

- isolated or biased evidence
- static outputs
- ad hoc interpretation

towards:

- clearer spatial context
- better-supported prioritisation
- more transparent reasoning
- more consistent use of habitat suitability evidence

## Non-goals

This project is not intended to:

- replace field survey
- provide definitive proof of species presence or absence
- automate ecological decisions
- remove the need for expert judgement
- become a general-purpose GIS platform

The aim is to support ecological judgement, not replace it.

## Data and attribution (to be refined)

- **Attribution:** Some form of attribution will be required (e.g. for data sources, models, or partners); exact wording and placement to be decided.
- **Records:** Record data are owned by the contributing groups; raw records cannot be shared. Aggregates (e.g. gridded or summarised) may be shareable where agreed.
- **Models:** We own the IP on the suitability models; the app consumes model outputs (COGs) produced elsewhere and added via admin.
